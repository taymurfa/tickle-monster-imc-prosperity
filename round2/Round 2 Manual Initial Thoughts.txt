Unlike Round 1, there is no single deterministic "right answer" for the Round 2 manual challenge.Round 1 was a math puzzle played in a vacuum against a frozen order book. Round 2 introduces a Game Theory (PvP) mechanic. Your final outcome depends not just on your math, but on predicting the psychological behavior of every other player in the competition.Here is a breakdown of why this round has high variability and the underlying mechanics you have to solve.The Mechanics of the FormulaYour final score is driven by this equation:PnL = (Research × Scale × Speed) − Budget_UsedLet's look at how each pillar behaves:1. Scale (The Linear Growth)How it works: It scales purely linearly. 0% investment = 0 multiplier. 100% investment = 7 multiplier.The Math: $\text{Scale} = 7 \times (\frac{\text{Percentage}}{100})$Strategy: Because it is linear, every percentage point you put in here gives you a consistent, predictable return.2. Research (The Logarithmic Curve)How it works: It uses a logarithmic function, meaning it has severe diminishing returns. The difference between 0% and 10% is massive, but the difference between 90% and 100% is barely noticeable.The Math: $\text{Research} = 200,000 \times \frac{\ln(1 + \text{Percentage})}{\ln(101)}$ Strategy: You absolutely must put some budget into Research to get off the ground, but maxing it out to 100% is incredibly inefficient compared to putting those later points into Scale.3. Speed (The Game Theory Trap)How it works: Your investment percentage does not give you a fixed number. It ranks you against every other player's Speed investment.The Math: The player with the highest Speed % gets a 0.9 multiplier. The lowest gets 0.1. Everyone else is scaled linearly between them based on their rank.Strategy: This is the variable that makes a "perfect" answer impossible. If you put 50% into Speed, and everyone else puts 51%, you get the lowest rank (0.1) despite spending half your budget. Conversely, if you put 1% into Speed, and everyone else puts 0%, you get the highest rank (0.9) for almost no cost.The Two-Step Optimization ProblemTo solve this, you have to break it into two steps:Step 1: The Deterministic RatioAssuming a fixed amount of budget leftover for Research and Scale, there is a mathematically perfect ratio to split between them to maximize Research × Scale due to the interaction between the linear and logarithmic curves.Step 2: The Speed Arms RaceYou have to guess the "meta."Will people just divide equally (33 / 33 / 33)?Will people try to "snipe" the 33% crowd by going 34%?Will people realize Research has diminishing returns, drop it to 10%, and dump the rest into Speed?
